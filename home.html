Problem

Though collagen has proven to be effective in creating cosmetic products, biomaterials, and a variety of other uses, its extraction method has a much darker, unknown side. Cattles and other livestock are often raised and slaughtered at young ages to extract the collagen. Not only does this raise the question of livestock ethics, it also has more serious implications like diseases that are born from slaughterhouses. Reports have shown that these slaughterhouses can breed diseases like mad cow disease, fascioliasis, tuberculosis, and more.

Listening to Our Community

Our goal for Human Practices was to ensure a safe, ethical, and effective solution that aligns with the values of our community. We surveyed hundreds of people of varying backgrounds to gather their opinions about our project and incorporated propositions into our project. Our team consulted material chemistry professors, industry researchers, doctors, and a bioethicist before creating our final project design.

Education and Communication

We held an immersive, virtual synthetic biology camp to educate middle and high school students. We also wrote and published a 100+ page, comprehensive biochemistry book in 5 languages and an audio translation for the visually impaired to foster greater youth interest in biochemistry and increase accessibility in the field. Our content is uploaded to large sites like YouTube and Amazon to provide free, accessible material at a global scale.

Maintaining Lab Safety

Throughout the course of our project, our main goal was to prioritise safety above all else, whether that meant in our procedures, practices, or within the final product itself. In the lab, we underwent extensive safety trainings, in addition to following safe practices when handling potentially dangerous chemicals. By doing so, we were able to create a quality result that reflects the thought and effort that went into it.
